In Pipeline

A Peek Into My Work

The road does not ask
where you have been —
only where your feet
are willing to go next.

And the silence between
two mountains
is not empty —
it is listening.

— From "Title of Poetry Book"

She had lived in that city long enough to know its moods — the way the old bazaar smelled different before rain, how the pigeons on the minaret always knew before anyone else that something was about to change. When the letter arrived, she read it twice, then folded it carefully along its original crease, as if putting a difficult thought back where it came from.

— From "Title of Novel"

About

One writer.
Many worlds.

Rakesh Sainik writes poetry and fiction that navigate the space between the interior and the open road — the private self and the landscape that shapes it. His work has been described as quietly relentless, drawing from travel, memory, and the textures of everyday life in India.

He is the author of one poetry collection and one novel, with two more works in progress. He also paints, occasionally, and believes the two practices are the same impulse wearing different clothes.
